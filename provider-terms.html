Provider Agreement

Last updated: February 17, 2026

This Provider Agreement ("Agreement") governs the terms under which you ("Provider", "you", or "your") register and offer services through the Fabric platform operated by Fabric Technology Ltd. ("Fabric", "we", "us", or "our"). By registering as a provider through the Fabric API or dashboard, you agree to be bound by this Agreement, the Terms of Service, and the Acceptable Use Policy.

1. Provider Registration

To offer services through Fabric, you must register via the /v1/providers/register endpoint or the Fabric dashboard. During registration you must provide:

  • A valid service endpoint URL that is publicly accessible and responsive
  • An accurate description of your service and its capabilities
  • Appropriate category classification for your service
  • Pricing information in USD or USDC
  • A valid Ethereum wallet address on Base for receiving payments

You represent and warrant that all information provided during registration is accurate, complete, and not misleading. You agree to promptly update your registration information if it changes.

2. Service Obligations

As a registered provider, you agree to:

  • Maintain your service endpoint in a functional and accessible state
  • Respond to routed requests in a timely manner consistent with your advertised performance characteristics
  • Deliver services that match your registered description and category
  • Not degrade service quality, increase prices, or change capabilities without updating your registration
  • Implement reasonable security measures to protect data received through Fabric-routed requests
  • Comply with all applicable laws and regulations governing the services you provide
  • Maintain any licences, permits, or authorisations required to offer your services

3. Trust Scoring

Your services will be evaluated and scored across five weighted dimensions:

  • Uptime (25%) — endpoint availability measured by periodic health probes
  • Latency (20%) — response time relative to category median
  • Success rate (25%) — percentage of non-error responses
  • Feedback (20%) — aggregated user ratings (1–5 scale)
  • Reputation (10%) — on-chain ERC-8004 attestation score

You acknowledge and agree that:

  • Trust scores directly affect your visibility in discovery results and routing priority
  • Scores are calculated algorithmically and Fabric does not manually adjust individual scores
  • Low scores may result in reduced routing traffic or removal from discovery results
  • Trust score history is recorded on-chain and is publicly visible and immutable
  • Fabric is not liable for any loss of business resulting from trust score changes

4. Payments and Settlement

Payments for services rendered through Fabric are processed as follows:

  • Users pay Fabric for routed requests based on the applicable pricing
  • Fabric takes a platform fee (as published on our pricing page) and remits the remainder to providers
  • On-chain payments via x402 are settled in USDC on Base directly to your registered wallet address
  • Fiat settlements (where available) are processed according to the payout schedule for your plan
  • You are responsible for any taxes, duties, or obligations arising from income received through the Service

Payment disputes must be raised within 30 days of the disputed transaction. Fabric will investigate disputes in good faith and may review transaction logs, quality data, and feedback to resolve them.

5. On-Chain Identity and Reputation

Upon registration, a FabricIdentity record may be created for your service on-chain. This record:

  • Is associated with your Ethereum wallet address
  • Contains your provider metadata, trust history, and reputation attestations
  • Is publicly visible on the Base blockchain
  • Is updated automatically as your trust score changes
  • Cannot be deleted or modified by Fabric once recorded on-chain (though Fabric may append deactivation records)

You consent to the creation and maintenance of this on-chain record as a condition of participating in the Fabric network.

6. Deactivation and Removal

Fabric may deactivate or remove your provider registration if:

  • Your trust score falls below the minimum threshold for an extended period
  • Your service endpoint is unresponsive for more than 72 consecutive hours without notice
  • You violate this Agreement, the Terms of Service, or the Acceptable Use Policy
  • Your services are found to be illegal, fraudulent, or harmful
  • Required by law, regulation, or legal process

You may voluntarily deactivate your provider registration at any time through the dashboard or API. Deactivation does not erase your on-chain reputation history, which remains publicly accessible.

7. Provider Data

By registering as a provider, you grant Fabric a non-exclusive, worldwide licence to:

  • Display your service name, description, and category in discovery results
  • Monitor your endpoint for performance metrics
  • Include your service in aggregated platform statistics
  • Record trust score data on-chain

This licence terminates upon deactivation of your registration, except for data already recorded on-chain.

8. Representations and Warranties

You represent and warrant that:

  • You have the legal authority to enter into this Agreement and offer the registered services
  • Your services do not infringe the intellectual property rights of any third party
  • You hold all necessary licences and authorisations to provide your services
  • You will comply with all applicable laws, including data protection, consumer protection, and financial regulations
  • You are not subject to economic sanctions and your services are not offered from a sanctioned jurisdiction

9. Limitation of Liability

Fabric's liability to providers is governed by the limitation of liability provisions in the Terms of Service. Additionally, Fabric is not liable for loss of routing traffic, loss of revenue, or business interruption resulting from trust score changes, platform maintenance, or the actions of other users or providers.
